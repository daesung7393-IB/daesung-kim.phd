About

Where policy meets strategy — and the public completes the cycle

I am a master's student in International Business at Yonsei University School of Business, working under Professor Soonkyoo Choe. My research sits at the intersection of policy, business strategy, and the public — three pillars I believe must operate in alignment for sustainable economic progress.

My conviction was crystallized by a simple yet powerful insight: policy success depends not only on institutional design but also on how clearly its intentions are conveyed to firms and citizens. Economics is not merely about theory or data — it is about creating conditions in which policy, enterprise, and citizens move in alignment. Economic progress relies as much on communication and public trust as on analytical rigor.

I hope to study how policy design and communication can shape institutional environments that enable a virtuous cycle: where governments guide firms toward sustainable growth, firms contribute to public welfare, and citizens reinforce confidence in the economic system. Through doctoral training, I aim to develop principles of economic governance grounded in trust, fairness, and shared prosperity.

Research Interests

Three research questions

🏛️

Policy, Public Understanding & Firm Strategy

How does public understanding and support of government policy influence firms' innovation, investment, and strategic activity? I examine the transmission mechanism between policy communication and corporate decision-making.

Policy DesignPublic TrustFirm StrategyCommunication
⚡

Political Risk, Institutions & MNE Behavior

How do geopolitical risk, institutional distance, and corruption shape MNE subsidiary network decisions? Current work focuses on entry-exit dynamics, political ties, and anti-corruption efficacy in emerging markets.

Political RiskGVCCorruptionInstitutional Theory
🔋

Catch-up Strategy & Korean MNEs

How do latecomer firms leapfrog technological frontiers? Case studies on LG Energy Solution and LG Electronics explore path-creating catch-up strategies that transformed Korean firms into global leaders.
